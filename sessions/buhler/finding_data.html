UBC Library Research Commons¶

A multidisciplinary hub that supports research and provides training in research-enabling skills.

Abacus¶

Abacus is a data repository hosted by UBC Library and used by SFU, UBC, UNBC, and UVic.

The collection is selective: data is acquired to support research and teaching needs.

Data examples from Abacus¶

  • Statistics Canada microdata (e.g 2016 Census PUMF)

  • Recordings from the Linguistic Data Consortium (e.g. TIMIT)

  • UBC LiDAR (2010, 2015) and orthophotos (2000-2020)

Abacus upgrade, August 2020¶

  • Cleaner interface

  • API access to data files, documentation, and metadata

  • Statistics Canada PUMFs freely available

Planet¶

The Planet company operates satellites that image the entire Earth landmass on a daily basis.

In 2020 UBC Library licensed Planet Imagery and Archive, giving UBC researchers and instructors access to imagery from the PlanetScope and RapidEye satellites.

Global, weekly, 5m resolution, multispectral imagery going back to 2009.

Geodisy¶

Geodisy is a spatial discovery platform for Canadian open research data that allows users to find data using a map instead of keywords.

Geodisy was developed by a team from UBC Library and UBC Advanced Research Computing (ARC) with funding from CANARIE.
